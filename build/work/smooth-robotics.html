Handover

June 2021

Smooth Robotics helps production companies automate their welding processes. Accompanied by a tablet, production workers are able to operate a welding robot within minutes.

Our role

Over a few weeks, we helped Smooth Robotics and their software engineers turn a proof of concept into a comprehensive design system.

Ultimately our contribution helped transcend the product experience and systemize software development through a well-documented design system.

Aligning with the product team

Partnering with an external design firm can be challenging for any in-house product team. To ensure excellent results, we practice an age-old human-centered principle: listening.

The starting point for all our design work is talking to the right people. In the case of Smooth Robotics, this meant an unparalleled opportunity to learn from experts.

Speaking with the right experts

We aligned technical requirements and constraints with their software engineering team. We discussed target audience, business strategy, and product-market fit with their Business Development Manager. We examined product vision and branding with their CEO.

Software demo as a starting point

After the debriefing, we jumped straight into our prototyping tools. Smooth Robotics already had a software demo, which became the basis for our exploration. We explored and iterated core functionality, user journeys, app feel, and modality.

Rapid prototyping and design principles

We knew our most difficult challenge was to demystify and decomplexify using robotics efficiently in daily production.

In collaboration with the product team, we established a few guiding principles. These principles (though never written down) informed our decision-making throughout the process and helped us attack the challenge.

Our principles

  • User adaptation is vital. We consistently need to deliver an approachable and easy-to-learn user experience.
  • Our users’ technical abilities vary. We need to prioritize accessibility and a high sense of security in our user interface.
  • Production efficiency is core. Our design decisions need to be inclusive to lower usage barriers.

Now, equipped with a shared set of design principles, we dove into our primary product feature: a timeline for building and timing movements performed by the robot arm.

Product design, systemized

The last week was all about look and feel. We designed a contemporary product identity capable of carrying its weight against competing software. Our product design also matured the user experience by making the interface easier to understand at a glance.

Besides the visuals, a product primarily consists of words. How do we remain consistent in our word choices? How do we address our users when something fails? Do we capitalize all words or not? What's our tone of voice? We created a living document to address all of this.

A living design system

A shared success criteria for the project was to make Smooth Robotics’ software engineers independent going forward. A well-documented design system became the solution.

We created a comprehensive design system that ensured a consistent experience across customizable modules and uniquely designed page templates. We documented everything from color to font size to word choice to technical behavior.

Obsessing over the tiniest details in the design phase typically results in an improved time-to-market with new functionality.
